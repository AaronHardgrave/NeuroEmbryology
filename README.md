# NeuroEmbryology: Interactive Neural Development Module

An interactive web-based learning module designed for first-year medical students to explore the embryological development of the nervous system. This module combines 3D anatomical models with clinical correlations and self-assessment questions to create a comprehensive, high-impact learning experience.

## üéØ Learning Objectives

After completing this module, students will be able to:
- Describe the key stages of neural tube formation and closure
- Explain the development of brain vesicles and their adult derivatives
- Understand ventricular system development and CSF circulation
- Recognize clinically-relevant congenital malformations and their embryological basis
- Apply developmental knowledge to clinical vignettes

## üåü Features

### Interactive 3D Models
- High-quality Sketchfab models from University of Dundee, CAHID
- Rotate, zoom, and explore anatomical structures at your own pace
- Visualize complex 3D relationships that are difficult to appreciate in textbooks

### Stage-by-Stage Timeline Navigation
- **Stage 1**: Neurulation (Weeks 3-4)
- **Stage 2**: Neural Tube Formation (Week 4)
- **Stage 3**: Brain Vesicle Formation (Weeks 4-5)
- **Stage 4**: Ventricular System Development (Weeks 5-8)

### Clinical Integration
- High-yield clinical correlations at each stage
- Real-world applications of embryological concepts
- Focus on USMLE Step 1-relevant malformations:
  - Neural tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly)
  - Holoprosencephaly
  - Tethered cord syndrome
  - Congenital hydrocephalus (aqueductal stenosis, Dandy-Walker, Chiari II)

### Self-Assessment
- USMLE-style clinical vignettes with detailed explanations
- Interactive answer selection with immediate feedback
- Comprehensive rationales linking developmental concepts to clinical scenarios

### Student-Friendly Design
- Time estimates for each section (5-6 minutes per stage)
- Clean, distraction-free interface
- Mobile-responsive for studying on any device
- Progressive disclosure to prevent cognitive overload

## üéì Recommended Usage

### For Individual Study
1. **First Pass (20-25 minutes)**: Go through all four stages sequentially
2. **Focus on weak areas**: Revisit specific stages as needed
3. **Test yourself**: Complete all clinical vignettes before exams
4. **Pre-exam review**: Quick 10-minute refresher focusing on clinical correlations

### For Flipped Classroom
1. **Pre-class assignment**: Students complete module before lecture
2. **In-class discussion**: Focus on complex clinical cases and student questions
3. **Post-class reinforcement**: Students revisit challenging sections

### For Small Group Learning
1. Work through one stage together
2. Discuss clinical vignettes as a team
3. Share different perspectives on 3D models
4. Quiz each other on key concepts

|-------|--------|----------------------|------|
| 1. Neurulation | Neural plate formation, neural tube closure, neuropores | Spina bifida, anencephaly, folic acid prevention | 5 min |
